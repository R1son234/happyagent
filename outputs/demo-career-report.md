## Interview Brief

### Project Pitch

Walk through the Product Launch Campaign as your flagship story. Start with the business goal and audience insight that drove the campaign. Describe how you coordinated product, design, ops, and channel partners naming specific coordination mechanisms (e.g., weekly syncs, shared content calendar, dependency tracker). Show the campaign artifacts you built (messaging framework, audience segments, content calendar, FAQ, social content) and how they connected to the narrative. Close with the metrics you tracked (exposure, CTR, lead conversion, activation, retention) and what you adjusted mid-campaign based on signals. End with a specific learning applied to the next campaign.

### Architecture Talk Track

For the Campaign Material Copilot project, frame it as a system built to solve a real marketing workflow problem: fragmented material across JDs, briefs, notes, and drafts. Describe the architecture as structured prompts plus local file templates plus traceable output reports. Emphasize that every recommendation is grounded in inspected material (not guesswork) and that repeatable checklists reduce unsupported claims. Connect this directly to the JD bonus signal around lightweight automation for marketing research, material organization, and reporting.

### Tradeoffs

- Speed vs. structure: Structured prompts and templates produce consistent, traceable output but take upfront design time. For fast-turnaround campaigns, a lighter-weight approach may be needed.
- Data breadth vs. depth: Tracking many funnel metrics (exposure, CTR, leads, conversion, activation, retention) gives a full picture but risks spreading analysis thin. Prioritizing 2-3 north-star metrics per experiment may yield sharper learnings.
- AI assistance vs. human judgment: AI tools accelerate research and copy generation but require strong editorial judgment to ensure brand voice and positioning accuracy. Over-reliance can produce generic content lacking channel-specific nuance.
- Small-batch vs. scaled experiments: Small-batch experiments reduce risk and are easier to manage, but may not reach statistical significance. Scaling up requires broader audience pools and longer timelines.

### Questions To Expect

- Walk me through a campaign you planned end-to-end. What was the goal, how did you measure success, and what would you do differently?
- How do you balance data-driven decisions with creative intuition when positioning a product or writing copy?
- Tell me about a time you managed a cross-functional campaign with competing priorities. How did you keep it on track?
- What AI tools have you used in marketing workflows? Give a specific example of how they improved your output or efficiency.
- How do you structure a post-campaign review so that learnings are actually reused by the team?
- Describe a growth experiment that did not work as expected. What did you learn and how did you apply that learning?
- Which marketing metrics do you prioritize and why? How do you avoid vanity metrics?
